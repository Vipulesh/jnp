Know More About Jaunpur





Description

Situated on the banks of river Gomti, Jaunpur is a remarkable mixture of the Ganga-Jamuni Tehzeeb. Jaunpur is a city and a municipal board in Jaunpur district in the Indian state of Uttar Pradesh. It is located 228 km southeast of state capital Lucknow.

Jaunpur is located to the northwest of the district of Varanasi in the eastern part of the North Indian state of Uttar Pradesh. Demographically, Jaunpur resembles the rest of the Purvanchal area in which it is located.

Historically known as 'Shiraz-e-hind', Jaunpur is a humbling town with grand architecture.
Gomti, Sai, Varuna, Pili, and Basuhi are the five rivers which make its land fertile.

There are a huge number of Tourist Attractions in Jaunpur. Every year thousands of visitors come from every corner of the world to visit this beautiful city of Jaunpur. The Tourist Attractions in Jaunpur include monuments like the Shahi Bridge and Yamdagni Ashram, museums like the Archaeological Museum, Botany Museum and Zoological Museum, holy places like the Atala Masjid, Jami Masjid, Lal Darwaza Masjid, Khalis Mukhlis Masjid, Shitla Choukiya Dham, Temple of Mihar Devi, Temple of Sheetla Devi, Sadar Imambara, Kadam Rasool, Panje Shareef and Trilochan Mahadev Temple.

My hometown will always have a special place in my heart, growing up in the city of Jaunpur was a humbling as well as exciting experience for me. The peacefulness, calmness and the serenity of living in this quaint town is something that I will strive to find somewhere else in the world. The varied mix of different cultures and heritages provide a unique perspective to our city.
